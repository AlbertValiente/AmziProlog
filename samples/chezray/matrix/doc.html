Matrices

Matrix algebra could be introduced to Prolog by implementing a data type matrix and extending the arithmetic operators to recognise it. The only drawback would be that applications would not be portable.

We present here some matrix primitives when there is no matrix type and matrices are represented as nested lists.

Let: [[A11, A12, ... , A1n], [A21, A22, ... , [A2n], ... [An1, An2, ... , Ann]] denote a matrix in row-major order

This implies that a simple list denotes a row vector and a list of row vectors is a matrix. By this definition a row vector is not a matrix and no denotation is provided for a column vector. However, a column vector can be represented by a matrix of singleton lists [[A1k], [A2k], ... , [Ank]] and similarly a row vector by:the matrix: [[Ak1, Ak2, ... , Akn]]. To perform matrix operations on vectors these are the denotations that should be used.

ijth/4

ijth(A, I, J, AIJ) AIJ is the element at the Ith row and Jth column of A.

rbind/3

rbind(A, B, C) appends the rows of B to the rows of A, to produce C, provided A and B are conforming matrices.

rbind(K, B, C) if K is a number, appends the rows of B to a conforming row of Ks to produce C.

rbind(A, K, C) if K is a number, appends a conforming row of Ks to the rows of A produce C.

cbind/3

cbind(A, B, C) appends the columns of B to the columns of A, to produce C, provided A and B are conforming matrices.

cbind(K, B, C) if K is a number, appends the columns of B to a conforming column of Ks to produce C.

cbind(A, K, C) if K is a number, appends a conforming column of Ks to the columns of A to produce C.

transpose/2

transpose(A, B) A and B are mutually transposed.

minor/4

minor(A, I, J, M) M is the minor of element A(I, J).

matAdd/3

matAdd(A, B, C) C is the sum of matrix A and conforming Matrix B.

matSub/3

matAdd(A, B, C) subtracts matrix B from conforming Matrix A to produce C.

matMult/3

matMult(A, B, C) C is the product of matrix A and conforming Matrix B .

matKMult/3

matKMult(K, B, C) C is the product of constant K and Matrix B .

detL/2

detL(D, N) N is the value of the determinant of D, obtained by Laplace expansion.

detP/2

detP(D, N) N is the value of the determinant of D, obtained by pivotal condensation

detS/2

detS(D, N) N is the value of the determinant of D, obtained by the sweep operation

adj/2

adj(A, AdjA) AdjA is the adjugate (adjoint) of A

matInv/2

matInv(A. B) A and B are mutually inverse.

Notice: the adjugate of A is the inverse of A multiplied by the determinant of A.

minor/4

minor(A, I, J, M) M is the minor od A(I,J).

minors/2

minors(A, M) is a matrix of all the minors of A.

.

sweep/4

sweep(K, Pivot, A, Sweep) Pivot is the Kth diagonal element and Sweep is the result of the sweep operation with that pivot.

sweeps/3

sweeps(A, D, B) B is the recursive sweep through all diagonals, and D is the determinant of A.

pivCon/2

pivCon(A, Vector) Vector is the pivotal condensation of A, as a column vector. If A is square Vector is one element.

There is a new arithmetic operator sub (precedence 200) which performs matrix subscription:

A sub [ I, *] returns the Ith row of A,

A sub [*, J] returns the Jth column of A,

A sub [I, J] returns the element of A at row I column J,

A sub [I] returns the Ith element of vector A regardless of whether it is a row or a column vector,

where I and J are integers.
